Background

From Family Office to Independent Advisor

Jonathan Perles comes from a background in family office and complex operations — environments where insurance isn't a checkbox, it's a load-bearing component of a larger financial and estate plan.

That background made something clear: the most consequential insurance gaps aren't usually about the wrong product or the wrong carrier. They're about the distance between how a policy is written and how it actually functions in the real-world context of a family, a business, or an estate.

Rexford Insurance Solutions was built to close that distance.

Why Rexford

The Gap Nobody Was Filling

Most brokers are good at placing coverage. Fewer are equipped to ask the harder question: when something goes wrong, will this policy actually do what we think it does?

For families navigating estate planning, business owners managing succession risk, and executives with complex exposures, the answer to that question matters more than the premium. Rexford was built for clients who understand that distinction — and want an advisor who does too.

How We Work

Four Things That Define Every Engagement

We Help You Uncover What You Already Know

Most clients already sense where the gaps are. Rexford's job is to ask the right questions and give you a clear answer — not to steer you toward a product that happens to be available.

Structure Over Product

The question isn't which policy to buy. It's whether the insurance structure integrates correctly with the estate plan, the business succession plan, and the broader financial picture.

Coordinated Across Disciplines

Insurance that isn't coordinated with legal, tax, and financial planning creates gaps. Rexford works alongside California estate planning attorneys, CPAs, and financial advisors — because siloed planning has consequences.

Long-Term, Not Transactional

Coverage placed today needs to perform years from now. That requires ongoing review, not a set-it-and-forget-it approach — especially as family circumstances, asset values, and tax laws evolve.
